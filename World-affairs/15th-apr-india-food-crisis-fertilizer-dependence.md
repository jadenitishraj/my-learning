INDIA'S FOOD CRISIS — FERTILIZER DEPENDENCE & GEOPOLITICAL RISK
│
├── The trigger
│   ├── US blocking Strait of Hormuz
│   ├── 33 km wide seaway
│   ├── 20 million barrels of oil per day
│   ├── 80% of oil heads to Asia
│   └── 50% of India's fertilizers pass through it
│
├── India's fertilizer dependence
│   ├── Every crop needs NPK
│   │   ├── Nitrogen = chlorophyll, photosynthesis
│   │   ├── Phosphorus = roots, flowering, fruiting
│   │   └── Potassium = immune system, disease protection
│   ├── India controls almost none of them
│   └── Six supply lines feed India
│       └── All fertilizer import lines
│
├── Where fertilizers come from
│   ├── Nitrogen (Urea)
│   │   └── Made from natural gas — imported
│   ├── Phosphorus
│   │   └── Imported from Morocco
│   └── Potassium (Potash)
│       ├── Canada, Russia, Belarus
│       └── India has zero leverage
│
├── The terrifying ratio
│   ├── 1951: 0.49 kg NPK/hectare → 536 kg grain
│   ├── 1970: 13.61 kg → 872 kg
│   ├── 1990: 67.55 kg → 1,380 kg
│   ├── 2010: 144.94 kg → 1,930 kg
│   ├── Today: 139 kg → 2,515 kg
│   ├── Yields up 5x
│   └── Fertilizer use up 280x
│
├── Green revolution
│   ├── Born by 1968
│   ├── Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh
│   ├── New crop varieties
│   ├── New irrigation
│   └── Increased fertilizer use
│
├── Why not organic
│   ├── Would cut yields 20-25%
│   ├── 280-350 million people starving
│   └── Not an option
│
├── Government subsidy
│   ├── Buys urea bag at ₹2,200
│   ├── Sells to farmers at ₹242
│   ├── Taxpayer pays ₹1,958 per bag
│   ├── Since Russia-Ukraine war: ₹6.77 lakh crore on subsidies
│   └── More than education or healthcare spend
│
├── Subsidy creating doom loop
│   ├── Farmers think more fertilizer = more yield
│   ├── Cheap fertilizer → overuse → dead soil
│   ├── Need more fertilizer every year
│   ├── More imports, bigger subsidy bill
│   └── More dependence on foreign countries
│
├── Punjab soil crisis
│   ├── Ideal NPK ratio: 4:2:1
│   ├── Actual Punjab ratio: 31.4:8:1
│   │   └── 8x more nitrogen than soil can use
│   ├── Problem 1: dead soil
│   │   ├── Kills soil microbes
│   │   ├── Reduces organic matter
│   │   └── Fertile lands becoming barren
│   ├── Problem 2: poisoned water
│   │   ├── Excess nitrogen → nitrate in groundwater
│   │   ├── 440 districts with high nitrate levels
│   │   ├── Cancerous
│   │   └── Blue baby syndrome
│   └── Problem 3: more fertilizer spiral
│       ├── Farmers forced to add even more
│       └── Cannot cut back without losing harvest
│
├── Solution: Nano Urea
│   ├── Urea granules broken to 30-50 nano particles
│   ├── Sprayed on leaves via drones
│   ├── Enters plant through stomata
│   ├── Cost comparison
│   │   ├── Regular urea bag: ₹2,200 (govt cost)
│   │   └── Nano urea bottle via drone: ₹500
│   ├── Replaces entire 45 kg bag
│   ├── Nitrogen efficiency: 30-40% → 80-90%
│   ├── IFFCO developed world's first nano urea
│   └── Haryana, UP, Andhra Pradesh deploying it
│
├── Business opportunity
│   ├── Drones, agri-tech, precision delivery
│   ├── Government actively looking to buy
│   ├── Can save thousands of crores per year
│   └── Three Ps: priority, paranoia, prohibition
│
└── Still unresolved
    ├── Phosphorus still imported from Morocco
    ├── Zero leverage for potash
    ├── Doom loop still spinning
    ├── Every kharif season without change = more poison
    └── If Strait of Hormuz stays closed
        └── Food crisis next season
